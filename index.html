Research Focus

I work across the full stack of aerospace communication systems — from RF front-end and antenna design (helical, Yagi-Uda, dipole) to software-defined radio (SDR)–based telemetry, tracking, and data downlink. My research bridges experimental and computational domains, combining ground-station development and field operations (NOAA/ISS reception, CubeSat balloon validation) with advanced signal processing and distributed learning.

I focus on robust high-mobility waveforms such as OTFS and OFDM, and on privacy-preserving modulation recognition using federated and split learning architectures. Through this integration of hardware design and AI-driven communication intelligence, my goal is to enable secure, high-throughput connectivity for CubeSat and UAV networks.

Research Interests
  • Automatic Modulation Classification (AMC) with deep learning under high mobility and low SNR.
  • OTFS/OFDM waveform design in the delay–Doppler domain for UAV/satellite links.
  • Federated and split learning for privacy-preserving communications intelligence.
  • SDR-based ground systems for LEO satellites and UAV telemetry, tracking, and data links.
  • Antenna design and RF front-ends for compact aerospace platforms.
